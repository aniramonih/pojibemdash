Tragic end in Pittsford: Community shaken by apparent Murder-suicide

Pittsford, – a quiet community, was shattered by a devastating incident on Tuesday night. Authorities were summoned to a residence on Tobey Road following a distressing call to 911. Inside the house, they discovered the lifeless bodies of Jasmine I. Adams, 33, and Andre M. Pellam, 47, both residents of Pittsford.

Chief Deputy Michael Fowler of the Monroe County Sheriff’s Office revealed that the authorities are investigating the incident as a possible murder-suicide. The initial concern was raised due to a domestic violence situation, and although the cause of death was not immediately disclosed, investigators are diligently working to determine the circumstances leading to this tragic event.

“We are praying for their families during this very trying time,” said Deputy Brendan Hurley, a spokesman for the sheriff’s office, offering condolences to the grieving families.

The tight-knit community is in shock, grappling with the loss of two of its residents. As investigations continue, neighbors and friends come together, supporting one another in the face of this heartbreaking tragedy. The details surrounding this incident remain a focal point for investigators, who are keeping all avenues open in their pursuit of understanding the events that led to this devastating loss.
