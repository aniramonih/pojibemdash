All you need to know about James Comey

James Comey is an American lawyer with a net worth of $16 million. In 2013, when going through the Senate confirmation process to become the head of the FBI, Comey claimed a net worth of $11 million.

Who is James Comey?

James Comey was born on December 14, 1960, in Yonkers, New York, to Joan and J. Brien. In the early 1970s, Comey’s family relocated to Allendale, New Jersey, where his father worked in corporate real estate and his mother as a computer consultant.

As a teenager, Comey attended Northern Highlands Regional High School. He went on to attend the College of William and Mary, where he graduated in 1982. Comey later enrolled at the University of Chicago Law School, where he earned his JD in 1985.

After leaving the FBI, Comey made a fortune making private talks, but the majority of his net wealth stems from his stint as general counsel and SVP at Ray Dalio’s investment firm Bridgewater Associates, which has been regarded as the world’s largest hedge fund.

Comey worked for Bridgewater from 2010 to 2013. In his final year at Bridgewater, he earned $6 million. He also received a $3 million profit participation payout the following year he left.

What is James Comey’s career?

After graduating from law school, Comey worked in Manhattan as a law clerk for US District Judge John M. Walker Jr. He then joined Gibson, Dunn & Crutcher’s New York office as an associate. From 1987 to 1993, Comey worked for the US Attorney’s Office for the Southern District of New York. While there, he was also the Criminal Division’s deputy chief.

Comey entered the Clinton administration in 1996 as the managing assistant US attorney in charge of the US Attorney’s Office for the Eastern District of Virginia’s Richmond Division. He also worked as the Senate Whitewater Committee’s deputy special counsel and was the lead prosecutor in the 1996 Saudi Khobar Towers bombing investigation.

In early 2002, Comey was appointed US Attorney for the Southern District of New York. During his nearly two-year tenure, he oversaw the indictments of Adelphia Communications founder John Rigas, Martha Stewart, Samuel Waksal, and Frank Quattrone, among others. In late 2003, Comey was appointed deputy attorney general.

During his time in this role, he sparked some controversy, particularly for his evasiveness over Bush’s domestic surveillance program and his support for a memorandum authorizing the CIA to employ torture. However, Comey opposed a second memorandum and later expressed disapproval of torture techniques.

After leaving the Department of Justice in the summer of 2005, Comey became Lockheed Martin’s senior vice president and general counsel. He held these posts until mid-2010 when he moved to become general counsel at the investment management firm Bridgewater Associates.

Comey departed Bridgewater in early 2013 and was later appointed senior research scholar and Hertog fellow at Columbia University Law School. He was also appointed to the HSBC Holdings board of directors.
