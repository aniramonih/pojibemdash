About Lauren Graham

Lauren Graham, an American actress and writer, was born on March 16, 1967, in Honolulu, Hawaii. Her most notable roles include Lorelai Gilmore in the TV show “Gilmore Girls” and Sarah Braverman in “Parenthood.” She also appeared in films like “Sweet November” (2001), “Bad Santa” (2003), and “Evan Almighty” (2007).

She completed her education at Langley High School in McLean, VA, Barnard College in New York City, and Southern Methodist University in Dallas, Texas, earning degrees in English and Acting.

Lauren began her acting career in the early 1990s with small roles in TV shows and movies before gaining widespread recognition through “Gilmore Girls.” She continued to work in TV and film, receiving accolades for her performances.

Lauren Graham’s Kids

Despite her role as a parental figure on screen, Lauren Graham does not have children of her own. She did not have kids with her former boyfriend Peter Krause, although she helped care for his 20-year-old son, Roman. Graham and Krause were in a relationship from 2010 to 2021 but did not have children together.

Lauren Graham’s Age and Nationality

Lauren Graham, born on March 16, 1967, is 56 years old and holds American nationality.

Lauren Graham’s Family and Parents

Lauren Graham’s parents are Donna Grant and Lawrence Graham. Her mother, Donna Grant, worked in fashion, while her father, Lawrence Graham, was involved in the candy industry. Following her parents’ separation, Lauren grew up primarily with her father. Her mother passed away in 2005.

She has a half-sister and a half-brother from her father’s second marriage and a British half-sister, Shade Grant, from her mother’s second marriage.

Lauren Graham’s Career

Aside from her acting career, Lauren Graham is also a successful writer, with her novel “Someday, Someday, Maybe” becoming a New York Times bestseller. She has authored two more novels and is recognized for her work as a producer.
