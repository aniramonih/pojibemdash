Career

The dire interest for automobiles birthed in Hennessey right when he was a young adult. He earned a significant amount through a construction business and invested the money to buy a Mitsubishi car (3000GT VR-4) and started racing right after, primarily inspired by C. Van Tune.

However, he was aware that racing might not fetch him sufficient money, and hence, he decided to start altering and tuning cars and further extending the same services to customers as well. A new business, Hennessey Performance Engineering, was born out of a rented garage in Texas.

The Houston based company primarily dealt in modifying cars, most of them being sport-utility cars. The vehicles were from numerous brands like McLaren, Porsche, Lotus, Jeep, Ford, Ferrari, etc., to name a few. They mainly focus on enhancing the performance of the mechanical system of the car.

In 2008, Hennessey went on to establish the Tuner School, a private institute that aimed at training technicians in vehicle tuning. The mechanics at Hennessey Performance Engineering are the tutors in this school. They also formed a YouTube channel that features videos of vehicles in action. HPE built and upgraded over 500 vehicles in a single year.

One of his cars, Venom GT, set a Guinness World Record for achieving a speed of 300 kmph in less than fifteen seconds.

Hennessey’s work and his company’s performance has been widely covered by some of the best publications in the world like Motor Authority, Road and Track, Car and Driver, Top Gear, Motor Authority, The Drive, Robb Report, etc.

He founded a company called the Lonestar Motorsports Park that lets drivers test and gauge their car’s performance.

In 2017, he founded another company, Hennessey Special Vehicles, which specialized in modifying and redesigning of the fastest race cars in the world. “Venom F5” was the venture’s first vehicle. HSV has a test track of its own, which it uses to gauge the speed of the cars.

Awards & Achievements

The American entrepreneur has won many accolades as a racer in his 20s and early 30s. The first races he won were the Pikes Peak Hill Climb, the Nevada Open Road Challenge the Bonneville Salt Flats, and the Silver State Classic in Nevada, in which he stood forth. These victories were convincing enough for him to pursue a career in the automobile industry.
