Is Todd Yakoubian Leaving KATV? Why is Todd Yakoubian Leaving KATV?

Is Todd Yakoubian Leaving KATV? Yes, the renowned meteorologist Todd Yakoubian is leaving KATV after 18 years. Explore the details and reasons behind this decision.

Is Todd Yakoubian Leaving KATV?

Yes, Meteorologist Todd Yakoubian, a familiar presence on KATV Channel 7 for close to eighteen years, revealed his upcoming departure from the station this week. In an announcement shared via video on both Facebook and Twitter on August 14, Yakoubian gracefully conveyed the news, refraining from specifying his final day at the station. Remaining with the team are fellow Channel 7 meteorologists Barry Brandt, Melinda Mayo, and James Bryant, individuals acknowledged by Yakoubian as "true professionals" whom he extended his gratitude to.

Yakoubian became a part of the KATV team in 2005, transitioning from WRCB-TV in Chattanooga, Tennessee. His academic background includes a broadcasting degree obtained from the University of Oklahoma, complemented by a broadcast meteorology certificate from Mississippi State. Additionally, he holds certification as a broadcast meteorologist from the American Meteorological Society.

Why is Todd Yakoubian Leaving KATV?

Meteorologist Todd Yakoubian, a familiar face on KATV Channel 7, has shared his decision to bid farewell to the station after a remarkable 18-year journey.

In a heartfelt Twitter video posted just before 8:30 p.m. on Monday, Todd expressed his gratitude, saying,“Soon, I will be leaving KATV Channel 7 and I want to thank KATV for 18 wonderful years.” 

Addressing the viewers who have faithfully tuned in, he conveyed his deep connection, remarking,“I feel like we have created a bond over the past two decades. You’ve trusted me during severe weather and you’ve trusted me with the forecast each and every day.”

While Todd couldn't yet unveil his future endeavors post-KATV, he assured his audience that he would do so "at the appropriate moment."

Who is Todd Yakoubian?

Hailing from Little Rock, Todd Yakoubian is a dedicated meteorologist who joined the KATV weather team in August 2005. Holding memberships in the American Meteorological Society and the National Weather Association, he earned their seals of approval. In May 2008, he was honored with the esteemed Certificate of Broadcast Meteorology (CBM) from the American Meteorological Society for his exceptional educational background, extensive knowledge, and effective communication skills.

Todd's journey began after graduating from Little Rock Catholic High School in 1992. He earned a degree in broadcast journalism from the University of Oklahoma in 1996, followed by further meteorology education at Mississippi State University, where he became a certified broadcast meteorologist. His television career started in Missoula, Montana, where he served as a morning meteorologist and later as chief meteorologist, braving a northern winter that brought 111 inches of snow. He even found time to learn snow skiing and fly fishing.

In Chattanooga, TN, Todd's pivotal role contributed to a meteorological team that secured the Tennessee Associated Press award for best weathercast two years in a row. His time there also included coverage of notable events like the Veterans Day tornado outbreak and the extensive flooding from Hurricane Ivan, and he met his future wife, Andria. With Andria, Todd is a proud parent to two boys, Blake and Preston.

At KATV, Todd's accomplishments include consecutive wins of the Arkansas Associated Press best weathercast of the year award for 2006 and 2007, along with runner-up positions in 2008-2011.

Beyond broadcasting, Todd engages viewers through his blog and social media, earning recognition as the Best Blog in Arkansas for two years (2009 and 2010) by the Society of Professional Journalists. Proud to contribute to Channel 7's legacy, he takes pleasure in providing accurate forecasts to fellow Arkansans. In his leisure, Todd enjoys fishing, supporting the Oklahoma football team, and cherishing time with his growing boys.
