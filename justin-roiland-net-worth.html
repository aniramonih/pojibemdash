Justin Roiland Net Worth 2024: The Rise and Fall of the Rick and Morty Co-Creator

Justin Roiland is a voice actor, animator, writer, producer, and director from the United States who rose to prominence as the co-creator of the hit Adult Swim cartoon sitcom Rick and Morty.

He also formed the animation firm Justin Roiland’s Solo Vanity Card Productions! and the video gaming studio Squanch Games, as well as co-creating the Hulu series Solar Opposites.

However, in 2023, he was accused of domestic abuse and sexual assault, which resulted in his dismissal from the majority of his enterprises. In this article, we will explore his biography, career, net worth, and controversies.

Career Highlights

In 2004, Roiland started working for the Los Angeles-based media collective Channel101, founded by Dan Harmon and Rob Schrab. He directed and acted in several film shorts and television shows, including House of Cosbys, The Sarah Silverman Program, and Acceptable TV.

Along with Ryan Ridley and Jackie Buscarino, he co-hosted The Grandma’s Virginity Podcast. Roiland’s first major voice-acting role was as Oscar in the 2010 Disney animated series Fish Hooks. He also contributed writing to the show, which lasted three seasons.

He then went on to voice a variety of characters on other animated shows such as Adventure Time, Gravity Falls, and The Cyanide and Happiness Show. Roiland’s success came in 2013 when he and Dan Harmon co-created Rick and Morty.

Roiland voices both the sarcastic, mad scientist and his trusting grandson in the program. The show immediately became a cult classic and a critical success, garnering numerous accolades and nominations. Roiland also worked on the show as a co-writer, executive producer, and director, and it was renewed for 70 more episodes in 2018.

In 2020, Roiland will co-create Solar Opposites with Mike McMahan. The plot revolves around a family of aliens who crash-land on Earth and attempt to blend in. Roiland does voice work for the primary character, Korvo, as well as minor characters. The program is currently available on Hulu and has been renewed for a fourth season.

Roiland also started his animation studio, Justin Roiland’s Solo Vanity Card Productions!, as well as Squanch Games, a video game studio. Accounting, Trover Saves the Universe, and High On Life are among the video games he has produced and voiced. He’s also worked on other animation projects like Blark and Son, The Vindicators, and Koala Man.
