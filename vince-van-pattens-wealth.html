Vince Van Patten: An Overview

Vince Van Patten, an American actor, commentator, and former professional tennis player, was born on 17 October 1957 in Bellerose, New York. He comes from Dutch, English, and Italian descent. As the son of Hollywood actor Dick Van Patten, Vince has been active in the industry since 1970.

With a notable presence in Hollywood and the world of tennis, Vince has built a considerable net worth. As of early 2016, his net worth is estimated at $2 million. His successful ventures in acting and professional tennis have been key contributors to his financial status. Additionally, his role as a commentator for the World Poker Tour has further fueled his wealth.

Vince Van Patten’s Net Worth: $2 Million

Having grown up in Bellerose and Los Angeles, Vince’s career took off when he began working as a child artist under his father’s representation. His journey as a child actor included appearances in various television shows and commercials. Furthermore, he also gained recognition through roles in movies such as “Yesterday”, “Rock ‘n’ Roll High School”, and “The Dirty Dozen: The Deadly Mission,” significantly impacting his net worth.

Besides his acting career, Vince made a name for himself in the world of tennis. As a professional tour tennis player, he achieved the Rookie Of The Year Award from the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP). He also competed against prominent figures like John McEnroe in events such as the US Open and Wimbledon, achieving top rankings. His tennis career has played a significant role in his financial success.

Vince currently serves as a commentator for the World Poker Tour, which is broadcasted on Fox Sports Network. His interest in poker developed at a young age, under the guidance of his father. His prowess as a poker commentator has elevated his status and led him to co-write a novel about Vegas poker with Robert J. Randisi, adding to his wealth.

In his personal life, Vince married actress Eileen Davidson in 2003, with whom he has one child. Prior to this, he was married to Betsy Russell from 1989 to 2001, with whom he shares two children. Vince continues to enjoy his career as a renowned commentator and television personality, supported by his current net worth of $2 million.
