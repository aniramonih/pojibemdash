What GVWR means?

Gross Vehicle Weight Rating GVWR Meaning and Regulations

The GVWR is the Gross Vehicle Weight Rating, or the most weight that your vehicle can safely handle. In the past, you may have seen “Gross Axle Weight Ratings” or GAWR, but the modern gross vehicle weight rating is more precise.

Why are trucks called half tons?

Where Does The Half-Ton Designation Come From? The half-ton name comes from the payload capacity of this truck class. Half-ton trucks used to have payload capacities of 1,000 pounds, or half a ton. Modern half-ton trucks exceed this payload capacity.

What happens if you tow something too heavy?

Exceeding what your vehicle is designed to tow can strain your engine and transmission, accelerate brake wear, damage your tires and even warp your chassis. This could in turn trigger catastrophic failure while driving and could lead to property damage or serious injury.

Can a Ram 1500 pull a fifth wheel?

Technically, the Ram 1500 models can be used for fifth-wheel towing, but there’s really no reason to do so. The Ram 1500 Classic has a maximum possible towing capacity of up to 10,680 lbs, while the standard Ram 1500 has a maximum towing capacity of up to 12,750 lbs when properly equipped.
