Early Life & Biography

Michael Connelly was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, on July 21, 1956, to parents W. Michael Connelly and Mary Connelly. He was the second oldest child. His family moved to Fort Lauderdale, Florida, when Michael was 12 years old. He completed his schooling from St. Thomas Aquinas High School.

His interest in crime and mystery escalated when he was 16 years old. This happened when one day, on his way home from his work, he witnessed a man throw an object into a hedge. He went there to investigate and found a gun wrapped in a lumberjack shirt. He informed the police, but the man was already gone. He completed graduation from the University of Florida, majored in journalism and creative writing.

Personal Life

During the early years of his career, in 1987, he moved to California with his wife, Linda McCaleb. They got married in 1984. She is a literary manager. They met each other while they were in college. They have one child, a daughter named Callie. The family is currently living in Florida.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

After he completed his degree in Journalism, he joined a newspaper company in Florida. He mainly wrote about crime. Along with this, he was involved in interviewing air crash victims. His article earned him a nomination for the Pulitzer Award. He was also offered a higher post in the Los Angeles Times as a crime reporter.

After working for three years at the Los Angeles Times, he wrote his first novel, The Black Echo. It was based on a true story and was published in 1992. This novel also featured LAPD detective Harry Bosch for the first time. This character has featured several times as a central character in subsequent books.

Some of his other books are Chasing the Dime, The Concrete Blond, Void Moon, The Black Eye, The Closers, etc. In 2003, he became the President of Mystery Writers of America. He maintained that post for a year, leaving the job in 2004.

Many of his novels, such as Blood Work and The Lincoln Lawyer has been adapted into movies. Some of his most recent books that were published are The Crossing and The Wrong Side of Goodbye. These novels were published in 2015 and 2016, respectively.

Awards & Achievements

Michael Connelly has won most of the major awards that are presented to mystery writers such as the Edgar Award, Anthony Award, Macavity Award, Shamus Award, Dilys Award, and many numerous awards. His novel The Black Echo won the Mystery Writers of America Edgar Award in 1992. In 2012 his book, The Black Box, won the most lucrative award, RBA Prize for Crime Writing. This award is worth €125,000.
