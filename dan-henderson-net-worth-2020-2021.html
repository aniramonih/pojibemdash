Early Life & Biography

Dan Henderson was born as Daniel Jeffery Henderson on 24th August 1970 in Downey, California, in the USA. He was born together with his brother Tom Henderson and their father, Bill Henderson, was among the coaches who assisted them in their wrestling while in high school. He grew up in Apple Valley, California, where he started wrestling at a young age.

He attended Victor Valley High School, where he participated with various championships and won a number of medals. He attended Cal State Fullerton and Arizona State University, where he continued with his wrestling career.

Personal Life

Dan Henderson is a married man and has three children from his marriage. He was previously married to Alison Henderson, but after their divorce, he married Rachel Malter.

In 2007 he was diagnosed with testosterone replacement therapy (TRT), but it was banned by UFC, and this made Henderson quit the TRT.

Career

Henderson began his wrestling career when he was IN High School and participated in 1987 and 1988 championships. He participated in the 1993 NCAA championships. At the University level, he became the champion of 1991, 1993 and 1994. He was also the national champion at his senior in 1993, 1994, and 1997.

During the Summer Olympics, he went out to represent the US in 1992 and 1996. He has also participated in two World Wrestling Championships in 1994 and 1997, where he managed to finish at 12th position and 7th position. He was one of the coaches of wrestling at Cerritos College in the 1990s. After his defeat in the Olympics of 2000 and his trial in 2001 at the world team, he decided to shift to an MMA career.

Henderson had already trained for mixed martial arts (MMA) IN 1997, and his first win was with Brazil Open Tournament. He participated in his second tournament with UFC and was able to defeat champions like Allan Goes and Carlos Newton. His third tournament was with Rings: the king of kings, where he won five fights out of the 32 competitors.

He joined Pride Fighting Championship in late 2000 and was the winner of welterweight in 2005. He returned to UFC on 8th September 2007, where he was participating in the heavyweight championship. He signed a four-fight contract with Strikeforce in December 2009, but he lost to Jake Shields. In his next matches, he was able to defeat other champions. He announced his retirement on 8th October 2016, but he later participated in UFC Hall of Fame on 5th July 2018, where he finished as the winner.
