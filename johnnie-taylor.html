Overall Meaning

The lyrics of Johnnie Taylor's "Soul Heaven" describe a dream in which the singer has the opportunity to attend an amazing concert in Soul Heaven. The first verse sets the scene, describing the crowded concert lineup that the singer came across, with all of The Bar-Kays tuning up on stage, Jimi Hendrix getting ready to play, and Sam Cooke singing "A Change Gonna Come" while Otis Redding was sittin' at the dock of the bay. The second verse continues with the fun that the concert attendees were having, dancing and practically standing on their chairs in excitement as Jr. Walker performed, and enthusiastically cheering on Jackie Wilson, Sammy Davis Jr., and Z.Z. Hill as they gave their performances. The third verse brings on the big names, with Ella Fitzgerald and Mahalia Jackson making an appearance backstage alongside Elvis Presley with his blue suede shoes. There's even mention of the fresh rain that fell near the end of the concert, which didn't stop the attendees from staying put to hear the amazing Z.Z. Hill give an encore performance of "Down Home Blues." The final verse really brings the show of legends to a finale, where greats like Al Jackson, Marvin Gaye, and King Curtis filled the stage, and Tupac and Biggie were the ones who closed the show, eventually leading the singer to wonder whether it was even real or a beautiful dream.


"Soul Heaven" is a heartwarming tribute to R&B and soul legends, and is perfect as a feel-good anthem, providing the listener an escape into a beautiful dream world. The lyrics reflect the joy that these icons have brought to the masses and how even in death they have continued to inspire generations of music lovers. Upbeat and reminiscent, the song is also full of references that any soul music fan would enjoy.
