Bio of Christie Ileto

Ileto was born and raised in the capital of the United States of America, Washington, DC. Moreover, she holds an American citizenship and belongs to African-American nationality. 

The star further graduated from the Medill School of Journalism. Later, she enrolled at Northwestern University, where she studied Mass Media and Communications.

Ileto besides started her career as a reporter for the 6abc for WJZ-TV in Baltimore, Maryland. When she was there, she extensively covered the arrest of Freddie Gray, a 25-years-old who died from spinal injuries while in police custody. 

For twelve hours of live broadcast, she gave an up-close look at the unrest fueled by his death to Marylanders and the nation.

Prior to her reporting in Baltimore, Ileto served in many news stations including WAFF in Huntsville, AL, and KOAT-TV in Albuquerque, NM. 

The journalist then served on CNN where she covered weather on how New Mexican were dealing with the the barrage of inclement weather that shut down a major interstate from Texas to Arizona. 

Currently, she serves as an action reporter for 6abc Action News since December of 2015. She is happy to return on the East Coast for reporting, which is closer to her family and friends.
