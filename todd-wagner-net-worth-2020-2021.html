Early Life

Career

In 1995, Todd Wagner started AudioNet along with Mark Cuban. They used to broadcast radio stations and live sports events on the internet. He became the chief executive officer of the company and soon expanded the business services of the company.

The company was renamed to Broadcast.com in 1998. It also became public. A year later, the company was sold for $5.7 billion to Yahoo!. Both Cuban and Wagner became billionaires, and the other 300 employees went on to become millionaires. Even after its sale, he continued to be in Yahoo! till 2000 and later quit it for focusing on other interests.

Following the success of Broadcast.com, the duo began to form Wagner/Cuban Companies. This also included the 2929 Productions. They went on to produce many successful films like ‘The Road,’ ‘Akeelah and the Bee’, ‘Enron: The Smartest Guys in the Room,’ and ‘Good Night, and Good Luck.’

Wagner has executive produced many films and television shows including ‘The Jacket,’ ‘The Benefactor,’ ‘The War Within,’ ‘One Last Thing…’, Broken English’, ‘What Just Happened?’, ‘Black Christmas,’ ‘Star Search,’ ‘Godsend,’ ‘Fast Track,’ and ‘Fay Grim.’

He has also done many other entertainment ventures. He is the co-owner of the production company called HDNet Films. He has also got interests in The Weinstein Company and Lions Gate Entertainment. Wagner has also got stakes in Dallas Mavericks. He serves on the board of trustees of the American Film Institute.
