Early Life

Richard Marvin “Dick” DeVos Jr. is the son of Richard Marvin DeVos and Helen June. He was born on 21 October 1955 in Grand Rapids, Michigan.

He completed his schooling from the public school system of Forest Hills. Later, he went to Northwood University for his BA degree in business administration. He also enrolled in Harvard Business School but did not complete.

In 1980, Dick DeVos got married to Betsy DeVos. His wife is the former chairperson of Michigan Republican Party. They have four children, Andrea, Elisabeth, Richard Marvin, and Ryan. They also have a grandchild. In philanthropy, he serves as the president of Dick and Betsy DeVos Foundation. He has also opened the West Michigan Aviation Academy.

Career

Dick DeVos was involved in his family business since he was a child. In 1974, he joined the Amway Corporation. He was holding positions in several divisions. In 1986, he became the vice president of the foreign operation of Amway.

In 1991, his family acquired the Orlando Magic of the National Basketball Association. He was later named as the CEO and President of Orlando Magic. He left Orlando Magic in 1993 to be the president of Amway. Later, he created Alticor as the subsidiary of Amway. He is also associated with the privately-held investment management firm called The Windquest Group.

In 1990, he was elected to the Michigan State Board of Education. He chairs with the Education Freedom Fund with his wife. DeVos was the co-chairman of the campaign “Kids First! Yes!”. In 2006, he unsuccessfully contested in the Michigan gubernatorial election.

DeVos released the book entitled ‘Rediscovering American Values’ in 1998. Currently, he serves as the chairman of RDV Corporation, Regional Air Alliance of West Michigan, West Michigan Aviation Academy, and Grand Action Committee.

He is also on the Governing Board of Orlando Magic since 1994. DeVos serves as the member of Michigan State Chamber of Commerce, Health Care Foundation, FAA Management Advisory Council, and Spectrum Health System.
