Allen Bestwick: Professional Life and Career

Allen Bestwick started his professional career at the age of 15. Initially, he did on-air work for his high school radio station in Coventry, Rhode Island.

Later, the journalist started announcing. In fact, he commentated at Seekonk Speedway.

And, this speedway is the track where his father raced.

Afterward, Allen was hired in New Jersey by the founder of MotorNet, Charlie Roberts. Primarily, he was hired to be the radio network’s Director of Affiliates and Associate producer.

Ultimately, in the later years of his professional career, Allen became a race reporter. Consequently, in 1986, he joined Motor Racing Network (MRN) to report the races.

Eventually, he became a co-lead announcer at MRN. And, he began announcing the sports events alongside Joe Moore and Barney Hall.

While continuing with MRN, Allen Bestwick took over for Ken Squier. And, he started working as TBS’s lead NASCAR commentator.

Later, NBC acquired rights to the second half of the NASCAR season. And Allen Bestwick joined the broadcasting. Also, the former NASCAR racer Benny Parsons and Wally Dallenbach Jr. joined the broadcasting team.

Joining the ESPN

Basically, joining ESPN was a turning point for the professional career of Allen Bestwick. Initially, he joined the NASCAR on ESPN package as lead pit reporter.

Also, he occasionally worked as a race coverage host and play-by-play announcer on Nationwide Series race. And, he occasionally performed the hosting duties in NASCAR Now.

Later, in 2008, the commentator was upgraded to full-time race coverage host. Simultaneously, he hosted a weekly Monday roundtable edition of NACAR Now.

Similarly, he hosted NASCAR Countdown as a lead pit reporter from 2008-2010.

Then, an announcement on July 20, 2011, revealed that Allen would replace Mary Reid for the 17 sprint Cup races. Actually, the races were hosted by Dale Jarett and Andy Petree.

Likewise, the announcement also stated that Nicole Briscoe would replace him as a host of NASCAR Countdown. And Nicole was going to host alongside Rusty Wallace and Brad Daugherty.

Additionally, Allen replaced Marty Raid as play-by-play announcer of the Sprint Cup Series races. And, the races were starting at the Brickyard 400.

Also, after Marty Raid was fired, Allen was called for hosting the remaining Nationwide series. And, this event happened in the series of 2013.

Becoming the lead announcer of ESPN

Later, ESPN’s NASCAR coverage ended after the 2014 season. Then, Allen Bestwick became the lead announcer for ESPN’s broadcast of IndyCar races.

Besides, he became the announcer for ABC in 2014 by replacing Reid.

Likewise, the sportscaster began to perform play-by-play duties for non-racing events. Such events also included college football matches.

However, Allen confirmed on April 28, 2017, that ESPN was releasing him. Actually, he was released after the 2017 IndyCar double-header Detroit Grand Pix.

Also, the commentator called this race his last Indy 500.

In February of 2018, ESPN announced bringing Allen Bestwick back to call the Indy 500 in 2018.
