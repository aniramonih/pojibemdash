Early Life & Biography

Shatta Wale was born on 17th October 1985 in Accra, Ghana. He is the son of Charles Nii Armah Mensah Sr. and Elsie Evelyn Avemegah. His father was a politician and also a businessman. His parents separated while he was young and was raised up by his father.

He attended Winneba Secondary School and later Seven Great Princes Academy. His father always wanted him to be a lawyer who made Shatta move to the streets and pursue his music career. He later went to Jamaica, where he polished his career.

Career

Shatta began his music career after completing high school and used to go by the stage name Bandana. His first single, which brought his recognition, is Moko Hoo featuring Tinny, a fellow Ghanaian musician. Some of his other songs under this name are No Problem and Obaa Yaa. He went missing for some time and then returned, having changed his stage name to Shatta Wale in 2009. He continued to release more music and was said to be the one who revived the Ghana dancehall, which had been abandoned for some time.

Shatta had songs such as Havana, Shatta City; Everybody Likes my Tin, and Dance Hall King, which was his biggest hit. He featured Davido in the song Wine Ya Waist. Music has taken him to different countries, including Canada, Europe, and the United States performing with P-Square and Flavor.

Two of his songs, Mahama Paper and Kakai, were used by the major political parties in Ghana to run the campaigns during the 2016 general elections. He was involved in the Covid-19 launch concert as headline artist on 13th April 2020, which was held with an aim to bring hope while fighting the disease.

Awards & Achievements

Shatta Wale has received many awards and nominations from his performance. Some of the awards include the Ghana Music Award for reggae song of the year 2014, Nigerian Entertainment Awards for African Artist of the year. He has also achieved a lot by reviving the music from Ghana, which has made him be celebrated by many.
