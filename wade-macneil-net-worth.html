Wade MacNeil Full Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings and Alexisonfire

Wade MacNeil, a staple figure in the Canadian music scene, co-founded Alexisonfire in the fall of 2001. His journey began when Dallas Green sought his help to book shows for his then-band, Helicon Blue.

Together with drummer Jesse Ingelevics, they laid the foundation for what would become a significant force in the post-hardcore genre.

Wade’s role as both a guitarist and backing vocalist was crucial in molding the band’s unique sound, characterized by a blend of aggressive hardcore and melodic elements.

Transition to Gallows

In 2011, Wade’s career took a pivotal turn when he joined the UK hardcore band Gallows, replacing Frank Carter as the lead vocalist. This shift came just after Alexisonfire announced their disbandment, making the timing serendipitous.

Wade’s debut with Gallows was marked by the EP Death Is Birth, signaling a new era for the band. His powerful vocals and dynamic stage presence helped Gallows maintain their fierce reputation in the hardcore punk scene.

Exploration with Black Lungs and Dooms Children

Aside from his roles in Alexisonfire and Gallows, Wade ventured into personal projects like Black Lungs, started as a side project but soon evolved into a significant endeavor showcasing his versatility in punk rock.
