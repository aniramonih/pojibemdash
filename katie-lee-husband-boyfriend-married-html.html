Meet Katie Lee's Spoiling Boyfriend/Husband-To-Be! Bio Reveals All Details

American chef and food critic, Katie Lee, having gone through a not so happy marriage, revealed her new love and headed to a bright future with her husband.

Standing at the height of 5’7, Katie Lee is a known chef and cookbook author who hosted the first season of Bravo’s Top Chef, a reality competition television series. The series has aired 231 episodes in 15 seasons.

Professional in Kitchen: TODAY’s Family Kitchen series showing Katie Lee going shopping at a local farmer’s market to prepare a farm-to-table meal in her outdoor kitchen. (Published on 3 August 2017)

Katie has appeared as a guest judge many times on Beat Bobby Flay, an American cooking competition show on the Food Network, which is taped in front of a live audience. She is also an editor of her culinary column, Katie’s Kitchen that is published every week in Hamptons magazine.

Katie Lee’s Bio (Age): Ethnicity & Upbringing

Born on 14 September 1981, Katie Lee Joel was raised in Milton, West Virginia. She is of Asian-American ethnicity born to parents Kim Becker and Steve Lee.

It looks like her parents made sure that she did get a good education. The 36-year-old chef studied journalism and food science graduating from Miami University in 2003 when she was also a member of Kappa Alpha Theta. She completed a semester at The British Institute in Florence, Italy.

Food Is Behind Katie’s Brag Worthy Net Worth 

According to sources, Katie has got an estimated net worth of $10Million, which doesn’t come as much surprise. From her two cookbooks, she has made a lot as a chef author. Her novel, Groundswell, also brought her tons of money as it sold many copies.

Katie earned a significant amount of money from her appearance as the host of Top Chef and as a special correspondent in the television newsmagazine, Extra. Currently, Katie is making money from co-hosting the talk show of Food Network's The Kitchen and the Cooking Channel’s Beach Bites with Katie Lee.

She worked in many restaurants and wine stores from the very beginning. She served as the house fishmonger after helping open Jeff and Eddy’s Restaurant in 2003.

Creating the foodie website OliveAndPeach.com, Katie gained some online audience in July 2005. It was in 2006 when she hosted Top Chef that Katie grasped massive attention.  

Katie published her first cookbook, The Comfort Table in April 2008, that had its second volume come out in 2009 with the name, The Comfort Table: Recipes for Everyday Occasions. She has also served as a food and lifestyle contributor for The Early Show.

How Did Foodie, Katie End Up Marrying Famous Musician Husband?

The food critic was married to the famous rock singer-songwriter, Billy Joels. There are only a few people who don’t know about Billy Joels. He’s a renowned music artist whose song, Piano Man, gained him much fame, making him the third-best selling solo artist in the US. He, however, was a chronic alcoholic and fell for Katie after seeing her in a hotel lobby. 

Katie and Billy met at the Peninsula Hotel, and after Katie's graduation from college, they gave their relationship a shot.

The pair got married in 2004 and shared a huge age difference. During the time of their marriage, Katie was just 23 while her husband was nearly double her age, at 55.  

Their marriage started going downhills in 2008 when Billy was on tour with Elton John. Katie in that time an up and coming culinary star would be splashed across the news for her lavish partying which led to her husband Billy suspecting of an affair.
