He co-owned the NBA team Oklahoma City Thunder, whilst also supporting charitable causes and giving back to society by donating to several Non-Government and Non-Profit Organizations. He had stakes in multiple enterprises such as businesses, restaurants, and real estate holdings.

Career

McClendon founded Chesapeake Energy Corporation along with Tom L. Ward. They utilized the not always agreed upon the technique of fracking to extract oil from the earth by pumping in fluids to bring the crude oil up. This, along with other cutting edge techniques to extract and process crude oil, made him known as a visionary leader in the ONGC sector. The company did very well when it went public in the year 1993, and in three years, the stocks went up almost three times in value, according to the Wall Street Journal.

He, being a proponent of Natural gas, made him set up the American Energy Partners LP or AELP along with others. This was a privately owned gas company based out of Oklahoma City.

Awards & Achievements

Aubrey was inducted into the Oklahoma Heritage Foundation’s Oklahoma Hall of Fame in 2007, was awarded the Chief Roughneck Award, United States Steel Corporation 2010, and also was given the Ernst & Young National Entrepreneur of The Year in Energy 2011.
