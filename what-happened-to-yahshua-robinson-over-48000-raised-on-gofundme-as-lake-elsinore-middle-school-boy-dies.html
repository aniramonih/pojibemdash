What Happened To Yahshua Robinson? Over $48,000 Raised On Gofundme As Lake Elsinore Middle School Bo

Many people are wondering what happened to Yahshua Robinson after his death. A GoFundMe has been opened to raise money for him.

Who is Yahshua Robinson?

12-year-old Yahshua Robinson, a student at Lake Elsinore Middle School, is said to have passed away on Tuesday as a result of being made to run outside in the blazing heat. According to several sources, Yahshua Robinson was hospitalised on Tuesday, August 29, after collapsing during his physical education lesson at Canyon Lake Middle School in Lake Elsinore.

Yahshua Robinson GoFundMe

The young kid died from cardiac arrest at the hospital after being made to run during physical education class in low- to mid-90-degree temperatures, according to a GoFundMe campaign set up to help the family with burial costs.

The boy’s bereaved family is reportedly struggling with the immeasurable loss of their cherished son and sibling, according to the crowdfunding campaign, which has already earned more than $48,000 of its $60,000 goal amount.

A fundraiser stated: “The agony of losing a child is indescribable, and as we wait with heavy hearts for the autopsy results, we are reminded of the unpredictability of life.” The page added, “We humbly ask for your assistance to help alleviate the financial strain this sudden tragedy has placed upon Yahshua’s family.”

Family react to Yahshua Robinson death

Since Yahshua Robinson passed away, his family is now blaming the physical education instructor, who is said to have disregarded the boy’s protests when he was made to run at Canyon Lake Middle School in Lake Elsinore. The family claimed that Yahshua Robinson’s death was caused in part by the school’s negligence.

Amarna Plummer, Yahshua’s aunt, told NBC Los Angeles that her nephew was forced to do laps in the sweltering heat as a punishment for not dressing properly for P.E. class. The family asserted that Yahshua repeatedly alerted the teacher to his distress while on the run, despite being in excruciating bodily pain.

The teacher allegedly ignored the boy’s protests up until his heat stroke led to his passing out.

Yahshua’s mother, a P.E. teacher at a separate school in the district, is also accused by Plummer of having cautioned Yahshua’s school administration about the dangers of holding courses outside in the sweltering heat. She stated:

“She informs the administration, ‘Do not let any children go out today for P.E.’ And what happened? She gets a call that he passed out on the field.”

A Lake Elsinore Unified School District representative expressed their sorrow at the student’s passing and said Yahshua “exhibited bodily distress.” In a letter distributed to district residents, it was stated: “Our teaching staff took prompt action to address the student and called emergency services, who were on campus within minutes. I provide this information to emphasise to our students and families that if ever a student were to demonstrate a need for attention, we will respond promptly to their needs and we will contact the family.”

Did his School issue any statement?

The school has declined to make any other comments, including on whether the incident was being looked into as a result of the family’s complaints.
