Early Life

Peter David Schiff was born to Irwin and Ellen Schiff in a middle-class Jewish family in Connecticut on March 23, 1963. His parents’ divorce made him move from Connecticut to Southern California. He owes his father big time for introducing him to the Austrian School of economic thought where his father previously served in the US Army during World War II and supported the US tax protester movement.

Schiff earned his high school degree from Beverly Hills High School, and he earned his Bachelor of Science and a double major in accounting and financing, in 1987, from the University of California. Tax Notes Today reported, in 2015, of Schiff’s accusation to the Federal Bureau of Prisons for inhumanely treating his father, who died in prison, where the Bureau did not allow the usual humanitarian release of his father.

Career

Schiff initiated his career as a stockbroker in the 1990s with Shearson Lehman Brothers Brokerage. He, within five years, obtained a thriving brokerage firm with a partner and called it Euro Pacific Capital where the office was located in Connecticut but had several branches in Florida, Los Angeles, New York City, etc. The primary strategy of the company emphasized long-term wealth saving where the emphasis was more on commodity and market-focused investment because of the decline in the US dollar.

He, henceforth, had massive success in his career because he found the Euro Pacific Bank which worked on a full reserve basis where it is licensed in St Vincent and the Grenadines. His Euro Pacific Canada Inc. deals in the capital market and brokerage services in Toronto where he also found Euro Pacific Asset Management which managed mutual funds of his clients with a long-term investment perspective in the global market.

His breakthrough did not stop at companies because of his adamant views on the US economy made him host ‘The Wall Street Unspun’ which was a weekly radio show, which lasted from 2005 to 2010. The ‘Peter Schiff Show’ on the terrestrial radio and internet, from 2010 to 2014, was also hosted by him where he expressed his views on current events, market activity, economic philosophy, etc. His thoughts lead into books where he wrote on the economy, especially the US economy.
