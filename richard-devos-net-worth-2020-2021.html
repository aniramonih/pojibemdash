Early Life

Richard DeVos was born in Grand Rapids, Michigan, in the year 1926. He was named Richard Marvin DeVos at birth by his parents, Simon Cornellius DeVos, who owned a small electrical business and Ethel Ruth Dekker. Richard went on to study at Calvin College. His career began with the United States Army Air Corps. In which he served during the Second World War.

Personal life

Richard DeVos was married to the late Mrs. Helen June Devos until 2017, which is the year she succumbed to death. The couple had four children together who have distinguished themselves in different fields. His son, Dan, is the owner of the “Griffins.” Richard Jr. was the Republican nominee for the Governor of Michigan and is presently married to the United States Secretary of Education, Betsy DeVos.

Age, Height, and Weight

Being born on 4 March 1926, Richard DeVos was 92 years old at the time of his death. His height was 1.7 m tall and weighed 75 kg.

Career

Richard DeVos founded Amway, along with Jay Van Andel. The company was restructured in the year 2000 as Alticor. Richard has also written a few books, which include “Compassionate Capitalism” and “Hope From My Heart: Ten Lessons for Life.” The latter book was written in relation to the by-pass surgery he had. He has co-authored a book about his success with Charles Paul Conn titled “Believe!”.

In 2014, he published his memoir, which is oddly named “Simply Rich.” He has been involved with a number of academic institutions as the member of their boards, some of which include Northwood University- Centre for National Policy and Constitution Centre of Philadelphia, which is a sort of museum dedicated to the constitution of the United States of America. He was also on the Board of the Christian Leaders Institute.

This organization provides Ministry training to bright young individuals, free of cost. He has also invested in several non-profit ventures, like Free Market Think Tanks and the Heritage Foundation. He has donated to the causes of health and education, among other fields. With his great interest in the field of sports, Richard was largely responsible for the starting of the sports management program at the University of Central Florida. This course was funded through the Richard and Helen Devos Foundation.

Over the course of his career, Richard Devos got increasingly involved in the political processes of the country, at least financially, if not otherwise. He is a big-time sponsor of the Republic Party and many different conservative causes, like the Focus on The Family and the American Enterprise Institute. He also became a close friend of Gerald and Betty Ford. He is famous for owning several sports teams in NBA, International Hockey League, and the American Hockey League.

Awards and Achievements

Richard DeVos has been honored with the William E. Simon Prize for Philanthropic leadership. He is the honorary trustee of the Gerald R. Ford Presidential Foundation. In 2012, Forbes noted Richard DeVos as the 60th Richest American and the 125th Richest Person in the World. However, earlier, he had also been ranked as the 10th Richest Person in America.
