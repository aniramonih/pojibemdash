Early Life and Career Beginnings

Christopher W. Wallace, born on October 12, 1947, in Chicago, Illinois, embarked on a successful journey in journalism. He laid the groundwork for his career during his time at Harvard University, where he honed his broadcasting skills at the campus radio station. Wallace’s passion for journalism led him to work for esteemed publications such as The Boston Globe, and he also gained valuable experience at a Chicago television station.

In 1975, Wallace joined NBC as a reporter, showcasing his dedication and talent for political reporting. He further solidified his reputation as he moved to WNBC-TV in New York and became a political correspondent for NBC News. Wallace’s ability to convey complex political stories earned him recognition and respect within the industry.

After his remarkable tenure with NBC, Wallace’s career took a significant turn when he joined ABC, where he served as a correspondent for 14 years. These years at ABC further elevated his profile and solidified his position as a prominent journalist.
